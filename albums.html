Yours Truly

"Yours Truly" is the debut studio album by American singer Ariana Grande, released on August 30, 2013, by Republic Records. The album features an R&B and pop sound, influenced by 1990s music. It includes hit singles like "The Way" featuring Mac Miller and "Baby I." "Yours Truly" received critical acclaim for Grande's vocals and its nostalgic production. It debuted at number one on the US Billboard 200, making Grande the first female artist to have her debut album debut atop the charts since Kesha's "Animal" in 2010. The album's success marked Grande's rise to stardom in the music industry.

My Everything

"My Everything" is the second studio album by American singer Ariana Grande, released on August 25, 2014, by Republic Records. The album features a mix of pop and R&B styles, showcasing Grande's vocal range and versatility. It includes hit singles like "Problem" featuring Iggy Azalea, "Break Free" featuring Zedd, and "Bang Bang" with Jessie J and Nicki Minaj. "My Everything" received generally favorable reviews from music critics, who praised Grande's vocal performance and the album's production. It debuted at number one on the US Billboard 200 and was eventually certified double platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA). The album solidified Grande's status as a pop superstar and further established her as a force in the music industry.

Dangerous Woman

"Dangerous Woman" is the third studio album by American singer Ariana Grande, released on May 20, 2016, by Republic Records. The album features a mix of pop, R&B, and dance-pop sounds, showcasing Grande's versatile vocal range and ability to blend different musical styles. The title track, "Dangerous Woman," serves as the album's lead single and sets the tone for the record with its empowering lyrics and sultry vibe. Other standout tracks include the infectious "Into You," the reggae-infused "Side to Side" featuring Nicki Minaj, and the emotional ballad "Sometimes." "Dangerous Woman" was a commercial success, debuting at number two on the Billboard 200 chart and spawning several hit singles. The album's success further solidified Grande's status as one of the leading pop stars of her generation, earning her critical acclaim and a dedicated fan base.

Sweetener

"Sweetener" is Ariana Grande's fourth studio album, released in 2018. The album marks a shift in Grande's musical style, incorporating elements of pop, R&B, and hip-hop. The album features production from Pharrell Williams, Max Martin, and others, contributing to its eclectic sound. The lead single, "No Tears Left to Cry," was praised for its uplifting message and catchy melody. Other notable tracks include "God is a woman," "breathin," and "thank u, next," which became anthems for self-empowerment and resilience. "Sweetener" received critical acclaim for its innovation and Grande's vocal delivery. The album debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 chart, becoming Grande's third album to achieve this feat. "Sweetener" earned Grande a Grammy Award for Best Pop Vocal Album, solidifying her status as a major force in the music industry.

thank u, next

"thank u, next" is Ariana Grande's fifth studio album, released in 2019. The album features a more personal and introspective tone, with Grande exploring themes of self-love, growth, and resilience in the face of adversity. The title track, "thank u, next," became an instant hit, topping charts worldwide and garnering praise for its empowering message of gratitude and moving on from past relationships. Other standout tracks include "7 rings," which celebrates independence and female empowerment, and "NASA," which uses space as a metaphor for needing personal space in a relationship. The album received widespread acclaim from critics, who praised Grande's vulnerability and maturity as an artist. "thank u, next" debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 chart, making it Grande's fourth number-one album in the United States.

Positions

"Positions" is the sixth studio album by Ariana Grande, released in 2020. The album features a blend of R&B, pop, and soul influences, showcasing Grande's versatility as a vocalist. The title track, "Positions," was released as the lead single and received critical acclaim for its sultry lyrics and smooth production. Other standout tracks include "34+35," a playful and provocative song about intimacy, and "POV," a heartfelt ballad about vulnerability and love. The album explores themes of love, empowerment, and self-discovery, with Grande's signature vocal harmonies and intricate melodies shining throughout. "Positions" debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 chart, making it Grande's fifth number-one album in the United States. The album received positive reviews from critics, who praised Grande's vocal performance and the album's cohesive sound.
